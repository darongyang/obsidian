

## **Problem 1**

**Assumptions:** steady 1-D flow, ideal gas for air, (p_1=p_2=p_{atm}) (statement says air leaves at atmospheric pressure), fuel is injected vertically (initial x-momentum of fuel ≈ 0), body forces neglected.


**English Solution:**

**Given**

- Section 1: $(T_1=20^\circ\mathrm{C}=293.15\ \mathrm{K}), (p_1=1\ \mathrm{atm}=101325\ \mathrm{Pa}), (u_1=150\ \mathrm{m/s}), (A_1=0.30\ \mathrm{m^2}).$
    
- Section 2: $(u_2=900\ \mathrm{m/s}), (A_2=0.20\ \mathrm{m^2}), (p_2=p_{atm}).$
    
- $Fuel/air mass flow ratio (m_{\rm fuel}:m_{\rm air}=1:40\Rightarrow m_{\rm fuel}=\dfrac{1}{40}m_{\rm air}.)$
    
- $Use (R_{\rm air}=287\ \mathrm{J/(kg\cdot K)}.)$
    

**1. Air density at inlet**  
$$
\rho_1=\frac{p_1}{R T_1}=\frac{101325}{287\times 293.15}=1.2043\ \mathrm{kg/m^3}.  
$$

**2. Air mass flow (inlet)**  
$$
\dot m_{\rm air}=\rho_1 u_1 A_1 =1.2043\times150\times0.3 =54.1948\ \mathrm{kg/s}.  
$$

**3. Fuel mass flow**  
$$
\dot m_{\rm fuel}=\frac{1}{40}\dot m_{\rm air}=1.3549\ \mathrm{kg/s}.  
$$

**4. Total exit mass flow**  
$$
\dot m_{2}=\dot m_{\rm air}+\dot m_{\rm fuel}=55.5496\ \mathrm{kg/s}.  
$$

**5. Momentum and pressure terms (x-direction)**  
General thrust (produced on fluid) from control volume:  
$$
T = \dot m_{2} u_2 - \dot m_{\rm air} u_1 + (p_2 A_2 - p_1 A_1).  
$$  
Since (p_1=p_2=p_{atm}),  
$$
p_2 A_2 - p_1 A_1 = p_{atm}(A_2-A_1)=101325(0.20-0.30)=-10132.5\ \mathrm{N}.  
$$

Compute the terms:  
$$
\dot m_{2}u_2 =55.5496\times900 \approx 50000.6\ \mathrm{N},  
$$  
$$
\dot m_{\rm air}u_1 =54.1948\times150 \approx 8129.2\ \mathrm{N}.  
$$  
So  
$$
T \approx 50000.6 - 8129.2 -10132.5 = 31732.9\ \mathrm{N}.  
$$

**6. Test-stand reaction (R_x).**  
The engine thrust acting on the test stand is balanced by the reaction ($R_x$). If we take positive ($R_x$) to the right (same direction as the arrow in the figure), then the required reaction magnitude is  
$$
\boxed{R_x \approx 3.17\times10^{4}\ \mathrm{N}\ \text{(to the right).}}  
$$

## **Problem 2**

**English Solution:**

We are given:

- Ambient conditions: $(T_0 = 230 ,K), (p_0 = 0.3 ,\text{atm} \approx 3.04\times10^4 ,Pa)$
    
- Mach numbers: $(M_0 = 0.2), (M_4 = 1.0)$
    
- Flow rate: $(\dot{m}_4 = \dot{m}_0 = 200 ,kg/s)$
    
- Temperature ratio: $(T_4/T_0 = 3 \implies T_4 = 690 ,K)$
    
- Exit pressure: $(p_4 = 1.3 p_0 = 0.39 ,\text{atm})$
    

Assume air as ideal gas:  
$(\gamma = 1.4), (R = 287 ,J/kgK).$

---

**Step 1: Freestream velocity**  
$$
a_0 = \sqrt{\gamma R T_0} = \sqrt{1.4 \cdot 287 \cdot 230} \approx 303 ,m/s  
$$  
$$
V_0 = M_0 a_0 = 0.2 \cdot 303 \approx 60.6 ,m/s  
$$

---

**Step 2: Exit velocity**  
$$
a_4 = \sqrt{\gamma R T_4} = \sqrt{1.4 \cdot 287 \cdot 690} \approx 528 ,m/s  
$$  
$$
V_4 = M_4 a_4 = 528 ,m/s  
$$

---

**Step 3: Thrust equation**  
Since nozzle exit pressure matches ambient (given (p_4 \approx p_0)), pressure thrust ≈ 0.  
$$
F = \dot{m}(V_4 - V_0) = 200 \cdot (528 - 60.6) \approx 93,500 ,N  
$$

---

**Step 4: Specific impulse (per unit air flow)**  
$$
I_{sp,air} = \frac{F}{\dot{m}_a g_0} = \frac{93500}{200 \cdot 9.81} \approx 47.6 ,s  
$$

---

✅ **Answer (Problem 2):**

- **Air specific impulse:** (47.6 ,s)

# **Problem 3**

**English Solution:**

We are given:

- Dry weight: $(73,400 ,kg)$
    
- Payload: $38,000 ,kg)$
    
- Fuel: $(51,000 ,kg)$
    
- Total mass: $(73,400 + 38,000 + 51,000 = 162,400 ,kg)$
    
- Engines: 4 turboprops, each thrust = (150 ,kN) → total thrust = (600 ,kN)
    
- Specific fuel consumption:  
    $$
    SFC = \frac{\dot{m}_f g_0}{T} = 6.5 \times 10^{-5} ,s^{-1}  
    $$
    

So:  
$$
\dot{m}_f = SFC \cdot \frac{T}{g_0}  
$$

---

**Step 1: Fuel flow**  
$$
\dot{m}_f = \frac{6.5 \times 10^{-5} \cdot 600000}{9.81} \approx 3.98 ,kg/s  
$$

---

**Step 2: Fuel endurance (time)**  
$$
t = \frac{51000}{3.98} \approx 12800 ,s \approx 3.55 ,h  
$$

---

**Step 3: Flight speed at Mach 0.7 and altitude 9000m**  
At 9 km: (T \approx 230K),  
$$
a = \sqrt{1.4 \cdot 287 \cdot 230} \approx 303 ,m/s  
$$  
$$
V = 0.7 \cdot 303 \approx 212 ,m/s  
$$

---

**Step 4: Range**  
$$
R = V \cdot t = 212 \cdot 12800 \approx 2.71 \times 10^6 ,m = 2710 ,km  
$$

---

**Step 5: Propulsive efficiency**  
Fuel power:  
$$
\dot{Q} = \dot{m}_f \cdot HV = 3.98 \cdot 45 \times 10^6 \approx 1.79 \times 10^8 ,W  
$$

Useful thrust power:  
$$
P_{thrust} = T \cdot V = 600000 \cdot 212 \approx 1.27 \times 10^8 ,W  
$$

Thermal efficiency = 85%, so available useful shaft power:  
$$
P_{avail} = 0.85 \cdot \dot{Q} = 0.85 \cdot 1.79\times10^8 \approx 1.52\times10^8 ,W  
$$

Propulsive efficiency:  
$$
\eta_p = \frac{P_{thrust}}{P_{avail}} = \frac{1.27\times10^8}{1.52\times10^8} \approx 0.84 , (84%)  
$$

---

✅ **Answer (Problem 3):**  
a) Range ≈ **2710 km**  
b) Propulsive efficiency ≈ **84%**

## Problem 4

**English Solution:**

**Given / assumptions**

- Flight Mach $(M_0=2.0)$
    
- Ambient (static) temperature $(T_0=-35^\circ\mathrm{C}=238.15\ \mathrm{K})$
    
- Ambient pressure $(p_0=15\ \mathrm{kPa}=15000\ \mathrm{Pa})$
    
- Fuel mass flow $(\dot m_f=1.4\ \mathrm{kg/s})$
    
- Equivalence ratio $(\phi=1.2)$
    
- Stoichiometric fuel/air ratio $(f_{st}=0.03) (mass basis)$
    
- Fuel $LHV (=37{,}800\ \mathrm{kJ/kg}=37.8\times10^6\ \mathrm{J/kg})$
    
- Exhaust speed $(V_9=1300\ \mathrm{m/s})$
    
- Nozzle perfectly expanded $(\Rightarrow p_9=p_0)$ (so no pressure thrust term)
    
- Air: $(\gamma=1.4,\ R=287\ \mathrm{J/(kg\cdot K)})$
    
- Definitions used:
    
    - mass air flow (\dot m_a=\dot m_f/f) with (f=\phi f_{st})
        
    - ram (inlet) drag (D_{\text{ram}}=\dot m_a V_0)
        
    - gross thrust (F_{\text{gross}}=\dot m_e V_9) with (\dot m_e=\dot m_a+\dot m_f) (thrust at zero flight speed)
        
    - net (uninstalled) thrust (F_{\text{net}}=F_{\text{gross}}-D_{\text{ram}}=(\dot m_a+\dot m_f)V_9-\dot m_a V_0)
        
    - thermal efficiency (\eta_{\text{th}}=\dfrac{\text{rate of KE increase}}{\dot m_f,\mathrm{LHV}}=\dfrac{\tfrac12\dot m_e (V_9^2-V_0^2)}{\dot m_f,\mathrm{LHV}})
        
    - propulsive efficiency (\eta_p=\dfrac{F_{\text{net}}V_0}{\tfrac12\dot m_e (V_9^2-V_0^2)})
        
    - overall efficiency (\eta_o=\dfrac{F_{\text{net}}V_0}{\dot m_f,\mathrm{LHV}})
        
    - TSFC (asked in (\mathrm{kg/hr\ per\ N})): (\mathrm{TSFC}=\dfrac{\dot m_f}{F_{\text{net}}}\times3600\ \mathrm{kg/(hr\cdot N)})
        
    - Range (Breguet form) (R=\dfrac{V_0 (L/D)}{c}\ln!\left(\dfrac{W_i}{W_f}\right)) where (c=\dfrac{\dot m_f g}{F_{\text{net}}}) (fuel weight flow per unit thrust), (g=9.81\ \mathrm{m/s^2}).
        

**Calculations**

1. fuel/air ratio  
    $$
    f = \phi f_{st}=1.2\times0.03=0.036.  
    $$
    
2. air mass flow  
    $$
    \dot m_a=\frac{\dot m_f}{f}=\frac{1.4}{0.036}=38.8889\ \mathrm{kg/s}.  
    $$
    
3. speed of sound and flight speed  
    $$
    a_0=\sqrt{\gamma R T_0}=\sqrt{1.4\times287\times238.15}\approx309.336\ \mathrm{m/s},  
    $$  
    $$
    V_0=M_0 a_0=2.0\times309.336\approx618.672\ \mathrm{m/s}.  
    $$
    
4. ram drag  
    $$
    D_{\text{ram}}=\dot m_a V_0=38.8889\times618.672\approx24059.5\ \mathrm{N}=24.06\ \mathrm{kN}.  
    $$
    
5. gross thrust  
    $$
    \dot m_e=\dot m_a+\dot m_f=38.8889+1.4=40.2889\ \mathrm{kg/s},  
    $$  
    $$
    F_{\text{gross}}=\dot m_e V_9 =40.2889\times1300\approx52375.6\ \mathrm{N}=52.38\ \mathrm{kN}.  
    $$
    
6. net (uninstalled) thrust  
    $$
    F_{\text{net}}=F_{\text{gross}}-D_{\text{ram}}\approx52375.6-24059.5=28316.1\ \mathrm{N}=28.32\ \mathrm{kN}.  
    $$
    
7. TSFC  
    $$
    \mathrm{TSFC}=\frac{\dot m_f}{F_{\text{net}}}\times3600=\frac{1.4}{28316.1}\times3600\approx0.1780\ \mathrm{kg/(hr\cdot N)}.  
    $$
    
8. efficiencies: first compute rate of kinetic energy increase  
    $$
    \Delta\dot E_{KE}=\tfrac12\dot m_e(V_9^2-V_0^2)  
    =0.5\times40.2889\times(1300^2-618.672^2)\approx(0.5_40.2889_ (1{,}690{,}000-382{,}708))\approx0.5_40.2889_1{,}307{,}292\approx 272{,}986\ \mathrm{W?}  
    $$  
    (numerical value below).  
    $$
    

Using the numbers:

- thermal efficiency  
    $$
    \eta_{\text{th}}=\frac{\Delta\dot E_{KE}}{\dot m_f\mathrm{LHV}}\approx0.4976\ (49.76%).  
    $$
    
- propulsive efficiency  
    $$
    \eta_p=\frac{F_{\text{net}}V_0}{\Delta\dot E_{KE}}\approx0.6652\ (66.52%).  
    $$
    
- overall efficiency  
    $$
    \eta_o=\frac{F_{\text{net}}V_0}{\dot m_f\mathrm{LHV}}\approx0.3310\ (33.10%).  
    $$
    

9. Range (given (L/D=14) and (W_i/W_f=1.3))  
    Compute (c=\dfrac{\dot m_f g}{F_{\text{net}}}) and then Breguet:  
    $$
    c=\frac{1.4\times9.81}{28316.1}\approx0.000485\ \mathrm{s^{-1}},  
    $$  
    $$
    R=\frac{V_0 (L/D)}{c}\ln!\left(\frac{W_i}{W_f}\right)  
    =\frac{618.672\times14}{0.000485}\ln(1.3)\approx4.685\times10^{6}\ \mathrm{m}\approx4685\ \mathrm{km}.  
    $$
    

**Final numeric answers (rounded)**

a) Ram drag (D_{\text{ram}}) = **24.06 kN**.  
b) Gross thrust = **52.38 kN**. Net (uninstalled) thrust = **28.32 kN**.  
c) TSFC = **0.178 kg·hr⁻¹·N⁻¹**.  
d) Efficiencies: thermal ≈ **49.8%**, propulsive ≈ **66.5%**, overall ≈ **33.1%**.  
e) Aircraft range (R) (with (L/D=14) and (W_i/W_f=1.3)) ≈ **4.69×10^6 m ≈ 4685 km**.

# Problem 5

**English Solution:**

We are asked to compute the stagnation temperatures (T_t), stagnation pressures (p_t), and relative cross-sectional area ratios (A/A_0) at different engine stations of an ideal turbojet engine.


**Given data**

- Specific heat ratio: (\gamma = 1.4)
    
- Freestream static temperature: (T_0 = 240\ \mathrm{K})
    
- Freestream Mach number: (M_0 = 1.0)
    
- Freestream static pressure: (p_0 = 0.20\ \mathrm{atm})  
    (also (p_9 = p_0), since the nozzle is ideally expanded)
    
- Compressor pressure ratio: (\pi_c = 16)
    
- Total temperature ratio (from combustor exit):  
    (\tau_\lambda = \dfrac{T_{t4}}{T_0} = 6 \implies T_{t4} = 6 T_0 = 1440\ \mathrm{K})
    
- Station Mach numbers (given in the problem):  
    $$
    M_1 = 0.70, \quad M_2 = 0.58, \quad M_3 = 0.90, \quad M_4 = 1.07  
    $$  
    (nozzle exit Mach (M_9) to be solved).
    
- Other data: (Q_R/(c_p T_0) = 135) (not directly needed here because (\tau_\lambda) is given).
    

We assume **ideal conditions**:

- Isentropic inlet, compressor, turbine, and nozzle
    
- No stagnation pressure losses in combustor
    
- Neglecting fuel mass flow in continuity and work balance
    

---

**Useful relations**

1. Stagnation temperature from static:  
    $$
    T_t = T \Big(1 + \frac{\gamma-1}{2} M^2 \Big).  
    $$
    
2. Stagnation pressure from static:  
    $$
    \frac{p_t}{p} = \Big(1 + \frac{\gamma-1}{2} M^2\Big)^{\tfrac{\gamma}{\gamma-1}}.  
    $$
    
3. Isentropic relation (between two stagnation states):  
    $$
    \frac{T_{t2}}{T_{t1}} = \Big(\frac{p_{t2}}{p_{t1}}\Big)^{\tfrac{\gamma-1}{\gamma}}.  
    $$
    
4. Area ratio (relative to station 0):  
    $$
    \boxed{  
    \frac{A_i}{A_0}  
    =\frac{p_{t0}}{p_{ti}} \cdot \frac{M_0}{M_i}\cdot \sqrt{\frac{T_{ti}}{T_{t0}}}  
    \left(\frac{1+\tfrac{\gamma-1}{2}M_0^2}{1+\tfrac{\gamma-1}{2}M_i^2}\right)^{\tfrac{\gamma+1}{2(\gamma-1)}} }  
    $$

**Step-by-step solution**

**1. Freestream (station 0)**

- Stagnation temperature:  
    $$
    T_{t0} = T_0 \Big(1+0.2 M_0^2\Big) = 240(1+0.2) = 288.0\ \mathrm{K}.  
    $$
    
- Stagnation pressure:  
    $$
    \frac{p_{t0}}{p_0} = (1+0.2M_0^2)^{3.5} = 1.2^{3.5} \approx 1.892929,  
    $$  
    $$
    p_{t0} = 1.892929 \times 0.20 = 0.378586\ \mathrm{atm}.  
    $$
    

Thus:  
$$
T_{t1} = T_{t0} = 288.0\ \mathrm{K},\quad p_{t1} = p_{t0} = 0.378586\ \mathrm{atm}.  
$$

---

 **2. Compressor (station 1 → 2)**

- Temperature ratio:  
    $$
    \tau_c = \pi_c^{(\gamma-1)/\gamma} = 16^{0.285714} \approx 2.208179.  
    $$  
    $$
    T_{t2} = \tau_c T_{t1} = 2.208179 \times 288.0 \approx 635.956\ \mathrm{K}.  
    $$
    
- Stagnation pressure:  
    $$
    p_{t2} = \pi_c \cdot p_{t1} = 16 \times 0.378586 \approx 6.057373\ \mathrm{atm}.  
    $$
    

At combustor inlet (station 3), we assume:  
$$
T_{t3} = T_{t2}, \quad p_{t3} = p_{t2}.  
$$

---

**3. Combustor (station 3 → 4)**

- Given:  
    $$
    T_{t4} = 1440.0\ \mathrm{K}.  
    $$
    
- Stagnation pressure unchanged:  
    $$
    p_{t4} = p_{t3} = 6.057373\ \mathrm{atm}.  
    $$
    

---

**4. Turbine (station 4 → 5)**

- Turbine work balances compressor work:  
    $$
    T_{t5} = T_{t4} - (T_{t2} - T_{t1}) = 1440 - (635.956 - 288.0) = 1092.044\ \mathrm{K}.  
    $$
    
- Stagnation pressure ratio:  
    $$
    \frac{p_{t5}}{p_{t4}} = \Big(\frac{T_{t5}}{T_{t4}}\Big)^{3.5}  
    = \Big(\frac{1092.044}{1440}\Big)^{3.5} \approx 0.379679.  
    $$
    

$$
p_{t5} = 6.057373 \times 0.379679 \approx 2.300683\ \mathrm{atm}.  
$$

---

**5. Nozzle (station 5 → 9)**

- At nozzle inlet:  
    $$
    T_{t9} = T_{t5} = 1092.044\ \mathrm{K},\quad p_{t9} = p_{t5} = 2.300683\ \mathrm{atm}.  
    $$
    
- At nozzle exit (static pressure equals ambient (p_9 = 0.20\ \mathrm{atm})):  
    Solve for (M_9):  
    $$
    \frac{p_{t9}}{p_9} = \Big(1+0.2M_9^2\Big)^{3.5},  
    $$  
    $$
    \frac{2.300683}{0.20} = 11.5034 = (1+0.2M_9^2)^{3.5}.  
    $$
    

Taking the 3.5-th root:  
$$
1+0.2M_9^2 = 11.5034^{0.285714} \approx 2.010,  
$$  
$$
M_9^2 = \frac{1.010}{0.2} = 5.05,\quad M_9 \approx 2.247.  
$$
