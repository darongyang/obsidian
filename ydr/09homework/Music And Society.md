## 中文
### 2.0 引入
刚刚介绍完了音乐在文化方面的影响，下面让我们来看看音乐在社会的影响。

音乐不仅仅是娱乐和文化的载体，它还可以通过增强社会凝聚力、反映社会现实、塑造公众意识等方式来对社会产生深远影响。音乐的力量在于它的激昂或悲怆的音律更容易带来人们情感上的共鸣。它能够以一种更加大众化和更加印象深刻的方式来引发人们对某个社会问题的反思和看法，从而改变人们的行动，最后影响社会的发展。

### 2.1 社会认同

首先，音乐在能够在社会认同上发挥作用，提高一个群体的凝聚力。在中国，耳熟能详的红色音乐。例如“我和我的祖国”。这首歌以第一人称的手法诉说了“我和祖国”息息相连、一刻也不能分离的心情，唤起人们的爱国精神与社会责任感，塑造了深厚的社会认同感和凝聚力。

在其他国家中，音乐在竞选集会和政治活动中具有强大的情感动员能力，能够迅速激发群众的情感共鸣，提升集会氛围，唤起选民的情感认同。例如《YMCA》在特朗普的竞选活动中成为了标志性歌曲。其轻快的旋律和易于传唱的歌词，帮助他与选民建立一种欢乐、积极的氛围，从而形成一种团结感。

### 2.2 社会导向

其次，音乐在社会导向上发挥作用，传递积极的社会情绪和价值观。2008年汶川地震发生后，许多音乐人为灾区创作了公益歌曲，如成龙的《生死不离》。这首歌慰藉了灾区人民的心灵，唤起了全国人民的团结和爱心，激发了社会各界对灾区的捐助与支持，成为了世界和灾区人民的情感桥梁。

类似的歌曲，《明天会更好》首先采用了轻快的旋律，表达了人们渴望世界和平的心声；又在后面通过高亢的旋律，凸现人们对明天美好生活的向往。

### 2.3 社会现实

此外，音乐还能够揭露社会现实，为一些弱势群体发声。它能够以更加直接或隐晦的方式反映社会中的不公、矛盾和问题。音乐能够为社会中的弱势群体发声，帮助那些无法通过主流渠道表达自身诉求的人群获得广泛的关注，让听众在音乐的同时意识到现实中的不公正现象。

反战是其中一个重要的角度。例如：经典的越战的小曲，Creedence Clearwater Revival的《Fortunate Son》。这首歌表达了对越战的不满和对阶级制度的批评。这首歌被广泛传唱，多次用于《阿甘正传》、《自杀小队》和《使命召唤：7黑色行动》等影视和游戏作品。

另外，还有现在正在发生的巴以战争，巴勒斯坦的人民也在用音乐《Oh Palestine，Oh Palestine》巴以战争，向国际获取更多的关注。

嘻哈音乐，例如《Alright》，源于美国贫民区，通过表达贫困、种族歧视、警察暴力等问题成为黑人社区的一种声音。

除了国外的音乐，华语乐坛上也有一些很火的歌，例如《杀死那个石家庄人》，促使我们去反思社会的现实。首先让我们一起看一段视频。

## English


### **2.0 Introduction**

We have just discussed the influence of music on culture. Now, let's explore its impact on society.

Music is more than just entertainment and cultural expression; it can also enhance social cohesion, reflect societal realities, and shape public awareness. The power of music lies in its ability to evoke emotional resonance through its stirring or melancholic melodies. It can provoke reflection and opinions on societal issues in a more popular and impressive way, ultimately influencing people's actions and society development.

### **2.1 Social Identity**

Firstly, music plays a role in social identity by strengthening group cohesion. In China, familiar patriotic songs like "My Motherland and I" connect individuals with their country, arousing patriotism and a sense of social responsibility, thus fostering a strong sense of identity and unity.

In other countries, music has an emotional mobilizing power during political campaigns and events, quickly arousing the audience’s emotional resonance and enhancing the atmosphere of rallies, leading to voter engagement. For example, "YMCA" became a signature song during Trump's campaign, with its cheerful melody and catchy lyrics helping to create a joyful and positive atmosphere, fostering a sense of unity.

### **2.2 Social Guidance**

Secondly, music conveys positive social emotions and values. After the 2008 Wenchuan earthquake, many musicians composed charity songs for the disaster-stricken area, such as Jackie Chan's "Never Apart." This song comforted the affected people, inspired national solidarity and love, and prompted donations and support from all sectors of society, serving as an emotional bridge between the world and the disaster-stricken areas.

Similarly, songs like "Tomorrow Will Be Better" use a lively melody to express people's longing for world peace and then shift to a high-pitched tune to highlight people's aspirations for a better life tomorrow.

### **2.3 Social Reality**

Moreover, music can reveal social realities and give voice to marginalized groups. It can reflect injustices, contradictions, and issues in society in a direct or subtle manner. Music can speak for those who cannot express themselves through mainstream channels, raising awareness among listeners about unfairness in reality.

Anti-war themes are one significant aspect. For instance, Creedence Clearwater Revival's "Fortunate Son" expressed dissatisfaction with the Vietnam War and criticized class inequality. Widely sung, this song was used in films and games like "Forrest Gump," "Suicide Squad," and "Call of Duty: Black Ops."

Additionally, the ongoing conflict between Palestine and Israel sees Palestinians using songs like "Oh Palestine, Oh Palestine" to gain international attention.

Hip-hop music, such as "Alright," emerged from American ghettos, addressing issues like poverty, racial discrimination, and police brutality, becoming a voice for the black community.

In the Chinese music scene, songs like "Kill That Shijiazhuang Man" prompt us to reflect on social realities. Let's watch a video clip together.

Universal Youth Hostel (also known as "Wan Qing") is a domestic rock band from Shijiazhuang. Regarding this song, I want to focus on the keyword "**silence**". The "killing" mentioned in the song is not actual violence but a symbol of **silence**.

The song tells the story of ordinary people living colorless lives, **silent** and marginalized in society. It does not directly criticize or promote any particular viewpoint but presents the **silent lives** of these individuals, leaving it to the listener to draw their conclusions.

From a social perspective, music brings their lives to the forefront, saying everything without saying anything at all. Even the simple presentation of reality is significant for them, and those in power **should not remain silent** when they see it.
